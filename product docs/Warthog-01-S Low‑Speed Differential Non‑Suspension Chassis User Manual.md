### 3.1 Environment

- Operate in an open area away from crowds, buildings, and sources of electromagnetic interference.
- Operate in areas below 5000 m altitude.
- Operate in environments with temperatures between -20°C and 55°C, under good weather conditions (not raining, foggy, snowy, lightning, high winds, or extreme weather).
- Operate in legal areas; do not drive onto motorways.
- When using RC remote control, operate within line‑of‑sight up to 300 m; when using industrial remote control, within 500 m. Ensure the surrounding area is open and safe.
- **For wall‑climbing robots, a safety tether must be installed at all times. Personnel are not permitted to stand beneath the robot during operation.**

---

### 3.2 Pre‑operation Checks

- Ensure the robot, remote controller, and mobile device have sufficient power.
- Ensure the battery is securely installed and the payload is properly fastened.
- Ensure tracks are intact and free of foreign objects; the drive and antenna are undamaged.
- Ensure the remote controller antenna has a full signal.
- Ensure all screws on the body and suspension are tight and secure.

---

### 3.3 Operation

- Always operate within visual line‑of‑sight.
- After power‑on, test left turn, right turn, forward, and reverse movements. If no abnormalities are found, proceed with normal operation.
- Novice operators should avoid rapid acceleration and deceleration.
- Power‑on sequence: ensure the remote controller is switched on first, then turn on the robot's main power and release the emergency stop.
- Power‑off sequence: turn off the robot chassis first, then the remote controller, to avoid signal loss that might trigger unintended modes.
- Beginners should first practise at low speed in an open area to become familiar with the controls. When moving the joysticks, push them smoothly and slowly. Because the driver's acceleration/deceleration ramp time is short, frequent sudden starts and stops will increase the load on the motors, drivers, and battery.
- You may simulate an emergency by turning off the remote controller while the chassis is moving. Practise pressing the emergency stop button promptly to enhance emergency response skills.

---

### 3.4 Maintenance

- After each run, inspect and promptly remove foreign objects from the tracks. Lubricate as per the maintenance manual.
- The robot, remote controller, and lithium battery are best stored long‑term at temperatures between 22°C and 28°C.
- For lithium battery maintenance, refer to the battery maintenance manual.
- When storing for an extended period, disconnect the battery charge/discharge connectors, ensure about 80% capacity, and charge once a month to prevent damage from deep discharge.

**Maintenance of drive chain (if equipped):**

a. The chain requires manual periodic lubrication – use an oil can or brush to apply oil once per shift.

b. Lubrication of worm gear reducer (if not equipped, disregard): After the first 500 operating hours, change the lubricating oil; thereafter, change oil every 6000 hours. The oil fill volume must be as specified. Do not mix oils of different grades; oils of the same grade but different viscosities may be mixed. Regularly inspect oil quality – if impurities, ageing, or deterioration are found, replace immediately. Oil should be added while the reducer is stopped; fill to approximately 3/4 of the reducer cavity volume. Use oil grade ISO VG320.

<summary><h2 style="display:inline; color: #F38181">🔋 4 Battery Safety Instructions</h2></summary>

Improper use, charging, or storage of batteries may lead to fire, property damage, or personal injury. Always follow these safety guidelines when using batteries.

### 4.1 Usage

- Before each use, ensure the battery is fully charged.
- Never allow the battery to come into contact with any liquid. Do not immerse the battery in water or get it wet. Do not use the battery in rain or humid environments. Water ingress may cause decomposition reactions inside the battery, leading to self‑ignition or even explosion.
- Use only official batteries provided by JCROBOT. If replacement is needed, please contact us for purchase information. We are not responsible for any battery accidents or faults caused by using unofficial batteries.
- Never use batteries that are swollen, leaking, or have damaged packaging. If any such conditions occur, contact JCROBOT or its designated supplier for further handling.
- The battery should be used in ambient temperatures between -20°C and 50°C. Excessive temperatures may cause fire or even explosion; too low temperatures will seriously damage battery life.
- Do not use the battery in environments with strong static electricity or magnetic fields, as this may cause the battery protection board to malfunction.
- Do not disassemble the battery or puncture it with sharp objects in any way – this may cause fire or explosion.
- The liquid inside the battery is highly corrosive. If leakage occurs, keep away from it and do not use the battery.
- If the battery has been subjected to severe impact or dropped, do not reuse it.
- Do not short‑circuit the battery terminals with wires or other metal objects.
- Do not strike the battery. Do not place heavy objects on the battery or charger.
- If the battery connector is dirty, clean it with a dry cloth. Otherwise, poor contact may cause energy loss or failure to charge.
- Do not continue using the battery when its charge drops below 10%; otherwise, the battery may be damaged.

---

### 4.2 Charging

- **Only use the dedicated charger provided by JCROBOT.** JCROBOT assumes no responsibility for any consequences resulting from the use of non‑official chargers.
- During charging, place the battery and charger on a concrete or other non‑flammable surface away from combustible materials. Monitor the charging process to prevent accidents.
- Before charging, ensure the battery power is switched off; otherwise, the power interface may be damaged.
- After charging is complete, disconnect the charger from the battery or robot. Regularly inspect and maintain the charger, and frequently check the battery exterior and all components.

---

### 4.3 Storage and Transport

- If the battery is low after use, or if it will not be used for more than 30 days, charge it to 30‑50% or more before storage. Otherwise, long‑term storage may damage the battery. Never store the battery fully discharged for long periods, as this may cause over‑discharge damage that cannot be recovered.
- Do not store the battery near heat sources, such as direct sunlight, inside a hot car, fire sources, or heating furnaces. The ideal storage temperature is 25°C.
- The storage environment should be dry. Do not place the battery in water or areas where it may get wet.
- Avoid mechanical impacts, crushing, piercing, dropping, or intentional short‑circuiting of the battery.
- Do not store or transport the battery together with sharp metallic objects.
- Never transport damaged batteries or batteries with more than 30% charge.

---

### 4.4 Disposal

- Always fully discharge the battery before placing it into designated battery recycling bins. Batteries are hazardous chemicals and must not be disposed of in regular trash bins.
- For details, follow local laws and regulations on battery recycling and disposal.

---

### 4.5 Maintenance

- Long‑term idle storage will affect battery performance.
- Recharge and discharge the battery approximately every 3 months to maintain its activity.
- Do not store the battery in environments where room temperature exceeds 50°C or drops below -20°C.

</details>

### 5.3 Remote Controller Operation (AT9)

This chassis is configured with the driver internal mixing mode. Channels 2 and 4 independently control straight motion and steering. The right joystick controls chassis movement in 8 directions: forward, backward, left, right, forward‑left, forward‑right, backward‑left, and backward‑right. All directions offer proportional control – the joystick deflection angle determines the movement speed.

**Do not make abrupt large movements, and avoid frequent sudden starts and stops.**

<img width="911" height="782" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/3d3ce1ce-a6c1-42c6-9d21-46379dd8a26d" />

a) **Joystick operation description**

① Right joystick – controls chassis movement:
- Push up: forward; pull down: backward (same for Ackermann, differential wheeled, and tracked vehicles).
- Push left: tracked vehicle pivots left; push right: pivots right (for wheeled vehicles: front wheels steer left/right).
- Push upper‑left: tracked vehicle moves forward‑left; upper‑right: forward‑right (same for wheeled).
- Push lower‑left: tracked vehicle moves backward‑left; lower‑right: backward‑right (same for wheeled).

② Left joystick – controls gimbal/payload (reserved):
- Push up: tilt up; pull down: tilt down.
- Push left: pan left; push right: pan right.
- Diagonal movements control combined pan/tilt motions.

b) **Receiver channel assignment**

<img width="818" height="329" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/480251fc-08b4-4b12-b1bc-39022f58cd48" />

**Settings (factory‑set):**
- In system settings, set the joystick mode to **Mode 4**.
- In model selection, choose **GLID (1A+1F)**.
- In servo travel, set elevator and rudder travel to **140%**.
- In servo phase, reverse channel 2 or 4 as needed to match vehicle movement direction.
- Disable flaperon mixing.
- Enable **V‑tail mixing**.
- Set Elevator 1 to +100%, Elevator 2 to -100%.
- Set Rudder 1 to +100%, Rudder 2 to +100%.

**Important:** The driver's internal mixing mode and the transmitter's V‑tail mixing should not be enabled simultaneously – only one of them should be active.

c) **For Ackermann wheeled chassis or pistol‑grip remote controllers**, follow the corresponding instructions for the relevant buttons; other aspects remain the same.

### 6.8 Operating Instructions

**1) Power‑on/off sequence**

First, turn on the remote controller power switch, then turn on the chassis main power and release the emergency stop. Wait for the receiver to establish communication (remote controller shows full signal). Then operate the remote controller to start movement.

**Power‑on sequence:** 1. Turn on remote controller first, 2. Then turn on chassis power.

**2) Remote controller operation**

This chassis uses the driver internal mixing mode (Mode 1). Channels 2 and 4 independently control straight motion and steering. The right joystick controls chassis movement in 8 directions, all proportionally controlled – joystick deflection angle determines speed.

Avoid abrupt large movements and frequent sudden starts/stops.

**3) Binding the remote controller and receiver**

a. Power on the receiver. Press and hold the binding button on the side of the receiver for 3 seconds – the receiver enters binding mode, with the red LED flashing slowly.

b. Power on the remote controller (ensure only one compatible remote controller is powered on nearby). Keep it close to the receiver, and wait for the receiver to receive the binding signal.

c. When the receiver red LED stays on, binding is successful.

**Note:** Binding is usually completed at the factory.

**4) Battery charging**

a. Unscrew the dust cap from the 2‑pin aviation charging connector on the chassis.

b. Plug the charger's 2‑pin aviation connector into the chassis (observe polarity: Pin 1 positive, Pin 2 negative).

c. Connect the charger to 220V AC mains.

d. When the charger indicator changes and the fan stops, continue balanced charging for 1 hour to complete the process.

e. Disconnect from AC mains, unplug the charger, tighten the waterproof cap, and store the charger properly for future use.

**Important:** Charging time = battery capacity / charging current + 1 hour (balancing time). Someone should monitor the charging process; do not overcharge for extended periods. Place the charger and chassis (battery) in a cool, well‑ventilated area, away from heat and fire sources.

---

### 6.9 Maintenance and Servicing

**1) Routine maintenance during operation**

a. **Cleaning:** Keep the exterior clean. Wipe painted surfaces with a soft cloth and neutral cleaner to avoid damage and scratches. Ensure all sliding surfaces, mating surfaces, and lubrication points are free of oil and dirt.

b. **Lubrication:**  
1. The reducer is factory‑filled with ISO VG320 lubricating oil; no initial refill is needed. After 500 hours of continuous operation, change the lubricating oil; thereafter, change every 6000 hours. Fill quantity 0.15L.  
2. The maximum allowable oil temperature for the reducer is 85°C; if exceeded, stop and inspect.  
3. If the reducer has been stored for more than 4‑6 months without the oil seal being immersed in oil, replacement of the oil seal is recommended.

c. **Adjustment:** Regularly inspect all fasteners for tightness; check for loose or missing screws to ensure safety, reliability, and control accuracy.

d. **Safety:** Regularly verify that controls are responsive and reliable, and that active and passive safety devices (such as collision bumpers, emergency stop switches, service brakes, parking brakes, and steering controls) operate correctly.

**2) Long‑term storage maintenance**

a. If the chassis will be stored for a long period, keep it in a cool, dry, and well‑ventilated environment. Protect from dust and keep components clean. Apply oil lubrication to moving parts and mating surfaces.

b. For extended storage, disconnect the battery charge/discharge connectors, maintain about 80% capacity, and check and recharge at least once a month.

c. When recommissioning the chassis, ensure all parts are well lubricated and the reducer oil has not aged or deteriorated.

**3) Replacement of wear parts and accessories**

a. **Bearings:** If wheel assemblies become loose, noisy, or jammed, replace the bearings with the same model (3205‑2RS, 4 pieces per chassis).

b. **Universal joint couplings:** Under continuous use, the universal joint coupling has a service life of approximately one year. If the universal joint coupling reaches the end of its service life due to wear or fails due to impact loading, contact the manufacturer to purchase replacement couplings.

**4) Common faults and troubleshooting**

| No. | Fault Symptom | Possible Cause | Solution |
| :---: | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | No response to remote control | Chassis main power off; emergency stop not released; remote battery low; receiver not bound | Turn on main power; release emergency stop; replace batteries; perform binding procedure |
| 2 | Chassis speed becomes very slow | Power battery low | Charge the battery |
| 3 | No movement and driver red LED stays on | Excessive ambient humidity | Dry the driver |
| 4 | Remote control range decreases | Receiver antenna loose or disconnected | Tighten the receiver antenna |

---

### 6.10 Transport Conditions

**1) Packaging dimensions:** Minimum carton approx. 750 × 700 × 450 mm.

**2) Handling requirements:**

a. The chassis is packed in a wooden crate with a wooden pallet base; total mass 50 kg. Use a pallet jack or forklift to move the pallet. When moving, do not tilt the crate more than 15 degrees. Ensure the fork fully supports the pallet to prevent tipping due to uneven centre of gravity. **Do not lift by the top cover or side panels** – the pallet may detach and damage the chassis.

b. The chassis may be driven onto the pallet using the remote controller. After placing it correctly, disconnect power, press the emergency stop, and preferably disconnect both discharge and charging connectors to prevent short circuits during transport.

c. When manually handling the chassis, ensure it is powered off.

**3) Transport environment notes:**

a. Do not invert the crate during transport.

b. Keep the crate dry.

c. Avoid strong vibrations during transport.
